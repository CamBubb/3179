Happiness Around the World

The happiness index is a comprehensive measure of overall life satisfaction, or quality of life, of the population of a country. This metric, which ranges from 1 - 10, is based off a range of factors such as:
    • Levels of social support
    • Individuals freedom
    • Perceptions of corruption
    • Generosity of the population
    • Level of optimism.

The map above provides an overview of the current state of the Global Happiness Index, as of 2022.

From this map, we can observe that
North America, North Eastern Europe, and Oceania show highest level of happiness, with scores ranging from 7 - 8, whereas Southern/Central Africa and Eastern Asia show lowest levels of happiness, with scores below 4.

Happiness Throughout Different Countries and Regions

It appears that there is a positive relationship between the Log GDP per Capita of a country and its happiness score. Inversely, it does not appear that confidence in governemnt has any relationship with happiness score.

Based on the bubble plot below, it is clear that as Log GDP per Capita increases (and therefore happiness score), the income level of countries directly increases in order from low income to high income.

The above ranged dot plot examines the change of Happiness Score of the top three countries since 2008 to 2022. From this, we can see that the countries tend to have a wider change over the specified time period, whereas the top three countries have a smaller spread. Finland and Zimbabwe appear to have the smallest change in Happiness Score over the time period.

Examining the distribution of Happiness Scores over broad regions, we notice that African countries are generally on the lower end of Happiness Scores, Asian countries are generally surrounding the lower/middle regions of the distribution, European countries tend to be in the middle to high portions of the distribution, which is similar to Latin American countries.

Imapct of Variables on Happiness

We can explore relationships between various features of a county, and its happiness score, over time.
As of 2022:

• We can see a negative relationship between perceptions of corruption in a country and its happiness score.

• There is no clear relationship between confidence in national governemnt and happiness score, however we may be able to identify clustering.

• Generosity shows a similar pattern to confidence in national government.

• However, we see a strong positive relationship between freedom to make life choices and happiness score.

• This is mirrored closely by healthy life expectancy at birth, social support AND log GDP per capita, as each of these variables have strong positive relationships with happiness score.
